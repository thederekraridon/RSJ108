Derek Raridon's Guide to a Better Interview

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to prepare for and conduct a solid interview

Assembly time: 2-3 Hours (Half for preparation, other half for conducting)
Success rating: 95% Chance of a successful and fulfilling interview

Are you having issues conducting interviews? Never feel prepared enough when talking with your person? Don't worry! This step by step guide will help you get that great interview, starting from finding your person of interest to how to end your interview!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Pen/Pencil
  • Notepad
  • Some form of communication
  • Internet access
  • Some water
  • A positive attitude

  1. Find a Person of Interest (or PoI)

    In order for an interview to be done, you need to find someone to interview. However, it can not be just an average joe from the street most times. Find a PoI, typically someone with a relevant title or connection to the topic, and set up an interview date

  2. RESEARCH!!

    You need to know what you are talking about to develop questions and have good flow in the interview. Take a bit of time to look up your topic online and learn more about it

    A reporter and her PoI getting prepared for an interview.

  3. Ask for consent to record

    This should happen both before and at the beginning of the interview. Asking for consent is how you save yourself from legal trouble

  4. Make sure to record their name and position on audio

    This helps with organization when writing and identification in case it is necessary for legal/libel trouble

  5. Show interest/Take notes

    Your PoI, about 99% of the time, did not reach out to you, you reach out to them. Make sure to show that you care about what they are saying with verbal/non-verbal ques. Taking notes is a great way to show this while keeping your interview organized

  6. Bounce off their answers

    The interview can go multiple ways, and some you may not have prepared for. Make sure to listen for things that you can ask on the spot questions for

  7. End the interview with a solid thank you and closing

    A simple thank you afterwards goes a long way. Adding to it is a closing, which you can ask things like if they want to read the draft over before printing or an invitation for a follow up.
